Welcome

This is a simple economics website dedicated to analyzing the trajectory of large currencies. This site enables those interested in economic data to perform economic analysis and see economic trends using interactive graphics.

About the Data

S&P500

{{listOfreturnHTML[0]}}
The S&P 500 stock market index measures the stock performance of 500 different large companies in the United States of America. It is one of the most commonly followed equity indices, and many consider it to be one of the best representations of the U.S. stock market.

Bitcoin

{{listOfreturnHTML[1]}}
Bitcoin is a cryptocurrency. It is a decentralized digital currency without a central bank or single administrator that can be sent from user to user on the peer-to-peer bitcoin network without the need for intermediaries.

Gold Spot Price

{{listOfreturnHTML[2]}}
The gold spot price is how much gold can be sold for on the spot, that very day. It is an indicator for the entire precious metals industry and a standard used by dealers when setting prices for consumers

13 Week US Treasury

{{listOfreturnHTML[3]}}
A Treasury Bill, or T-Bill, is short-term debt issued and backed by the full faith and credit of the United States government. The 13 week Treasury bill is debt obligation with a maturity of 13 weeks.

FAQ

Why do certain results in my query have "No Data" / Why are certain dates in my results omitted?

Stocks do not report prices on Saturdays and Sundays, however, certain datasets report "null" values for these days, whereas others do not. Other datasets, such as bitcoin, report data on every day of the week. To ensure clarity in our query results, we do not omit any of the data from our datasets.

Where is this data coming from?

For this project we pulled our data from Yahoo Finance. We cleaned the datasets up, and store them on our web server to be delivered to you.

What kind of regression analysis does your query perform?

Our analysis uses the Sci-KitLearn Python Module to perform linear regression analysis using the chosen datatypes as the regressor and regressand.
